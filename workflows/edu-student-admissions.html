01 — The Problem
Admissions Running
on Paper & WhatsApp

Nigerian schools and training institutions process hundreds of applications through a patchwork of WhatsApp messages, paper forms, and spreadsheets. Deserving students fall through the cracks. Great talent is lost to disorganisation.

📱
WhatsApp Applications
Prospective students send qualifications via WhatsApp. Admissions officer screenshots and saves to a folder. Application sits unseen for days. Student follows up — still no response. Student enrols elsewhere.
📋
Manual Document Checks
Admissions officer manually reviews WAEC results, birth certificates, and reference letters. Checks authenticity by eye. 30 minutes per applicant × 200 applicants = 100 hours of admin every admissions cycle.
📊
No Pipeline Visibility
How many applications received? How many are complete? How many offers sent? How many accepted? How many spaces remain? The admissions manager doesn't know the answer to any of these without manually counting.
🔄
Slow Offer Letters
Application reviewed. Offer approved. Admissions officer types the letter, formats it on letterhead, emails it. Takes 2 days per offer. Top students receive 3 offers in that time and choose the institution that responded first.
✅ n8n handles the complete admissions pipeline: digital application, document upload and verification, scoring, offer letter generation, acceptance tracking, and enrolment confirmation.
