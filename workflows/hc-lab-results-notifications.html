01 — The Problem
Lab Results Lost
in the System

A lab result sitting unretrieved for 48 hours can be the difference between early intervention and a medical emergency. Yet most hospitals have no automated pipeline from lab to patient and doctor.

📞
Patients Calling In
"Is my blood test ready?" "Can you check my malaria result?" Reception fields dozens of these calls daily. Staff interrupt clinical work to check a lab system that could simply notify the patient automatically.
⏰
Critical Values Missed
Dangerously high potassium. Critically low haemoglobin. These results require immediate clinical action. But if the doctor only checks results at their next consultation — which might be tomorrow — a patient is at risk.
🔤
Unreadable Reports
"FBS: 11.2 mmol/L (Ref: 3.9–5.5)" — what does that mean to a patient with no medical training? Most patients receive their results on paper, understand nothing, and either panic or ignore them entirely.
📁
No Continuity of Care
Lab result from one visit not linked to the same patient's next visit. Doctor orders the same test twice. History of results never visualised as a trend. Each visit starts from scratch rather than building on a record.
✅ n8n monitors the lab system, delivers results to patients with GPT-4o plain-language explanations, and flags critical values to doctors in under 5 minutes — automatically.
