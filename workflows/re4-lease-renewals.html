01 — The Problem
Leases That
Expire Unmanaged

For property managers handling 20+ units, lease expiry management becomes a constant game of catch-up. Leases lapse, tenants go month-to-month, rent reviews are missed, and surprise vacancies create cash flow gaps.

📅
The Spreadsheet Problem
Lease expiry dates in a spreadsheet nobody checks until it's too late. Manager discovers a lease expired 3 months ago, tenant is now month-to-month without a rent review. Revenue opportunity missed.
📤
Reactive, Not Proactive
Renewal conversation started when the tenant gives notice to leave — not 90 days before when there's still time to negotiate, retain them, and plan for replacement if they're leaving. Too late = too expensive.
💸
Missed Rent Reviews
Tenancy agreement includes an annual rent review clause. Manager forgets to apply it. Tenant has been paying the same rent for 3 years while market rates increased 25%. ₦200k/year in lost revenue per unit.
📝
Manual Agreement Renewal
New agreement typed up every renewal. Same template, same clauses, different names and dates. 45 minutes per renewal. For a portfolio of 30 units, that's 22 hours of annual admin that automation handles in seconds.
✅ n8n monitors every lease, starts the renewal process 90 days before expiry, applies rent reviews at the right date, auto-generates new agreements, and tracks the entire pipeline.
