// The Challenge — Nigerian O&G HSE Incident Reality
Slow Incident Reporting Creates Regulatory and Human Risk
⏰
Late Incident Reporting
DPR requires preliminary notification within 24 hours of a HIGH severity incident. At Chevron Escravos, paper-based incident report takes 48–72 hours to reach HSE coordinator. Regulatory window already missed.
→ DPR fine exposure per late notification
📊
No Severity Triage
Field worker reports gas leak via WhatsApp group with 50 members. No one knows whether to classify it HIGH or MEDIUM. Response is delayed while supervisors debate the classification. Emergency protocol not triggered.
→ Avg 2hr delay before response protocol
📋
Investigation Not Launched
After incident closes, root cause investigation is supposed to start within 48 hours per DPR regulations. At Agip Brass terminal, investigation team assigned 2 weeks later — after multiple reminder emails.
→ 60% of investigations start late
📁
Incomplete Audit Trail
Incident data lives in 4 different places — WhatsApp, email, paper form, and Excel. When DPR auditors request the full incident register, HSE team spends 3 days reconstructing timeline from multiple sources.
→ 3-day audit response scramble
