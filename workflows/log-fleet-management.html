01 — The Problem
Fleet That Breaks
Without Warning

A logistics company's biggest variable cost — after fuel — is unplanned vehicle downtime. Breakdowns that could have been prevented with scheduled maintenance cost 3–5x more than the service would have.

🔧
Reactive Maintenance
Trucks serviced only when they break down. Oil changes missed. Tyre pressure not checked. Brake pads worn past safe limit. Each breakdown costs: recovery, lost revenue, emergency repairs, and customer trust.
📋
Manual Service Tracking
Service records in a manual logbook per vehicle. Nobody checks until something goes wrong. Fleet manager doesn't know which vehicles are due for service, which roadworthiness certificates are expiring, or which tyres are near-end.
⛽
Fuel Leakage
Driver reports 80 litres consumed. GPS shows 400km driven. Average consumption for that truck type is 0.15L/km = 60 litres expected. 20-litre discrepancy. Without automated tracking, this theft or waste goes undetected.
📊
No Utilisation Data
Some trucks run 5 days a week. Others sit idle 3 days. No routing optimisation. Asset under-utilisation directly reduces revenue per vehicle. Without data, fleet expansion decisions are guesswork.
✅ n8n monitors every vehicle's km, service schedule, fuel consumption, and document expiry — sending alerts before problems occur and flagging anomalies that signal misuse or mechanical issues.
