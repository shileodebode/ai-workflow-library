01 — The Problem
The Paper-Based
Waiting Room

Nigerian hospitals and clinics lose enormous operational capacity to manual intake processes. Patients fill paper forms at the front desk while qualified clinical staff wait. It doesn't have to work this way.

📋
Paper Form Bottleneck
Patients arrive, collect a form, fill it at a shared desk, hand it back. Receptionist manually transcribes into the system. 15–20 minutes per patient. At peak hours, queues form before a single doctor has seen anyone.
🔁
Repeat Data Collection
Returning patient fills the same form again because records can't be found or weren't digitised last time. Patient frustration rises. Time wasted. Same information collected 5 times across 5 visits.
🗂️
Fragmented Medical History
Patient's previous visit notes are in a manila folder somewhere in the records room. Doctor sees a new patient every time. Critical allergy or medication history missed. Clinical risk, not just inconvenience.
📊
No Queue Intelligence
Receptionist manages queue manually. No visibility of how many patients are waiting, which doctors are available, or which cases are urgent. Walk-ins and scheduled patients treated identically.
✅ n8n sends a digital intake form to patients before arrival. Medical history auto-loaded for returning patients. Queue managed intelligently. Doctors see full patient context before walking into the consultation.
